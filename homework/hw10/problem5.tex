  \item Suppose $y_1, y_2, \ldots, y_n$ follow the distribution $y_i\stackrel{iid}{\sim}Geo(\theta)$, with the default prior $\theta \sim Be(1,1)$. Derive the posterior distribution $p(\theta | y)$.

\ansfont{
For the prior  $\theta \sim Be(a,b)$,
\[ \begin{array}{rl}
p(\theta|y) &\propto \prod_{i=1}^n p(y_i|\theta)p(\theta) \\
&\propto  \left(\prod_{i=1}^n(1-\theta)^{y_i-1}\theta \right) \theta^{a-1} (1-\theta)^{b-1}\\
&\propto  (1-\theta)^{n\bar{y}-n}\theta^n\theta^{a-1} (1-\theta)^{b-1}\\
&\propto  \theta^{a+n-1}(1-\theta)^{b+n\bar{y}-1}\\
\end{array} \]
Thus the posterior is a $Be(a+n, b+n\bar{y}-n)$. Letting $a=b=1$ gives a $Be(1+n, 1+n\bar{y}-n)$ posterior distribution.
}
